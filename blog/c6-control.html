Search Inside Yourself by Chade-Meng Tan - Chapter 5

  • When programming, what are some of your triggers? What causes a disproportionate emotional response?

    • I feel negative emotions when I face problems I don't know how to solve. Usually when I cannot manage to find solutions, I tend to feed the monster. I feel like I'm failing and doubt myself whether I can successfully finish the bootcamp while I'm struggling even at phase 0. However, learning about problem solving strategies and knowing I have my cohort peers and teachers to ask questions help me deal with these negative feelings better.

  • How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?

    • Stopping allows me to stop feeding the monster with things I probably don't need to worry about, and acknowledge that I am experiencing the negative emotion. Breathing helps me calm down and step away from the negative feeling, and notice why I'm feeling this way. Reflecting on my own reaction from another person's perspective stops me from reacting which forms my responds to the situation more mature and helps me deal with the situation better.

    I find reminding myself of the Siberian North Railroad exercise difficult which makes it hard to practice it every time I face the negative emotions. But I know the more I do the exercise and mindfulness meditation, the better I will be with the practice :)
